About This Map

GRIZZLY BEARS (Ursus arctos) are protected in the contiguous United States under the federal Endangered Species Act. The federal species recovery strategy encourages demographic connectivity between currently isolated Grizzly Bear Recovery Areas and a key recovery goal is reestablishing a breeding population in the Bitterroot ecosystem through natural immigration. We used the locations of 362 verified grizzly bear den sites, Maxent species distribution modeling, ArcGIS and resource selection functions to predict 21,091 km2 of moderately and highly suitable denning habitats. Moderately suitable habitat was more abundant while highly suitable habitat comprised < 5% of the study area. Our best model with variables slope, trended elevation, distance to open roads and ski resorts, land cover and distance to water best explained suitable denning habitats in western Montana and northern Idaho and was the most consistent with the literature review. Our results and dispersal distance analysis lend support to the demographic model for connectivity. Independent of other factors we estimate there is available suitable denning habitat for more than 500 grizzly bears in the Bitterroot analysis area. We suggest areas north of the Selway-Bitterroot Wilderness be added to the Bitterroot Grizzly Bear Recovery Area.

Bader M, Sieracki P. 2021. Grizzly bear denning habitat and demographic connectivity in northern Idaho and western Montana. Flathead-Lolo-Bitterroot Citizen Task Force Technical Report 02-21. Missoula, MT 33p.
