About This Map

This map presents a Grizzly Bear Cumulative Effects Model developed for commenting on the Soldier-Butler EA to evaluate habitat conditions in the context of proposed management actions and existing levels of human access. The analysis focuses on the relationship between grizzly bear habitat use and proximity to open roads and motorized trails. Core habitat is defined as areas at least 500 meters from open roads and motorized routes, representing conditions with reduced human disturbance and lower risk of displacement or mortality. The model incorporates spatial data on transportation networks to identify and map the distribution of core and non-core habitat across the Nine Mile Demographic Connectivity area. This provides a cumulative assessment of how existing and proposed road systems and motorized use influence habitat security and landscape connectivity. This map is intended to inform review and comment on the NEPA analysis by illustrating the extent to which the proposed action may affect grizzly bear habitat effectiveness and the availability of secure core areas..
